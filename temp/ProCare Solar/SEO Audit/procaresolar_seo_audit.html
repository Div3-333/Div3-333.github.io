Critical issues — fix first
JavaScript-only rendering (no HTML content served)
The server returns a nearly empty HTML shell — only the text "ProCareSolar" is in the source. The site is a client-side SPA, so Googlebot sees blank pages on first crawl. This is the single most damaging technical issue and explains why only 2 pages are indexed. Fix: add server-side rendering (SSR) or a prerender/CDN layer (e.g. Wix SSR is built-in, or use Prerender.io).
Only 2 pages indexed by Google
A site:procaresolar.com.au search returns only the homepage and /get-quote. Service pages, about page, and any other content are invisible to search engines. Every service needs its own crawlable, indexable URL.
No Google Business Profile signals visible
For a local trade service in Melbourne, the Google Business Profile (GBP) is often responsible for more leads than the website. No GBP listing or review count is surfacing in search results. Without this, the site won't appear in the local map pack — the 3 results shown above organic listings.
No reviews or trust signals
Competitors like Mr Sparkle show "300+ 5-star Google reviews" directly in search snippets. ProCareSolar has no visible review count. In a trust-dependent trade category, this is a conversion and ranking gap.
On-page issues
Page title includes "Solar Panel Installation" — inaccurate
The title tag shown in search is "ProCareSolar | Solar Panel Installation | Solar Panel Cleaning…". The business doesn't appear to install panels. This creates a relevance mismatch with the actual service and could attract the wrong search intent. Recommended title: "Solar Panel Cleaning & Maintenance Melbourne | ProCareSolar".
No dedicated service pages
Services like drone thermal imaging, robotic cleaning, bird proofing, inverter upgrades, and inspections are all described on one page. Each service should have its own URL (e.g. /solar-panel-drone-inspection-melbourne) to rank for long-tail search terms independently.
Grammar error in visible content
"Our specialised robotic equipments" — "equipment" is uncountable in English and does not take a plural. Small errors like this reduce E-E-A-T (expertise/authority/trust) signals Google uses to evaluate content quality.
No suburb-level landing pages
Competitors rank by serving pages like /solar-panel-cleaning-east-melbourne, /solar-panel-cleaning-bayside etc. A Melbourne trade business can capture high-intent local searches through geo-targeted pages for the suburbs it services.
No structured data (schema markup)
No LocalBusiness, Service, FAQPage, or Review schema is evident. Schema helps Google display rich results (star ratings, FAQs, service details) directly in the SERP, improving click-through rate without requiring a rank change.

Recommended action plan — priority order
1. Fix JS rendering — enable SSR or prerendering
Nothing else matters until Googlebot can read your pages. If using Wix, enable Wix's built-in SSR. If custom-built, add a prerender service or move to a server-rendered framework.
2. Optimise and verify Google Business Profile
Add all services, photos, opening hours, and service area suburbs. This is free and can produce ranking results within weeks for local pack searches.
3. Build individual service pages with on-page SEO
One page per service with unique H1, meta title, meta description, 400+ words of content, and an FAQ section. Target pages: cleaning, inspection, drone imaging, bird proofing, inverter repair, robotic cleaning.
4. Add LocalBusiness + Service schema markup
Implement JSON-LD schema on all pages. Include NAP (name, address, phone), service types, and area served. Add FAQPage schema to each service page.
5. Actively collect Google reviews
Send a follow-up SMS/email after every job with a direct Google review link. Aim for 20+ reviews within 3 months. Reviews are a direct local ranking signal and drastically improve click-through rates.
6. Create suburb landing pages
Add pages for the top 10–15 suburbs you service (e.g. St Kilda, Fitzroy, Essendon, Box Hill). Each page targets "[service] [suburb]" keywords and links to the relevant service pages.
7. Build local backlinks
Get listed on hipages, ServiceSeeking, Houzz, TrueLocal, and local business directories. Reach out to solar panel installers who could refer maintenance customers and link to your site.
8. Start a content blog
Publish 1–2 articles/month targeting informational queries: "how often should you clean solar panels in Melbourne", "signs your solar inverter is failing", "drone vs manual solar inspection". These build topical authority and attract inbound links.
